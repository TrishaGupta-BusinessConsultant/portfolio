AI Strategy · Digital Transformation · Public Sector

AI Adoption Strategy : Public Local Authority

Confidential Public Sector Client · UK

Mapped 10 council workflows end-to-end to identify where AI could create real value and where it couldn't. Delivered a phased implementation roadmap with GDPR governance controls that projected £80,000+ in annual savings and 3,000+ staff hours reclaimed.

£80k+ savings 3,000+ hours reclaimed GDPR governance Human-in-the-loop safeguards
↗
The problem

A Public Local Authority wanted to adopt AI but had no framework for deciding where it was appropriate, safe, or valuable. The risk of getting it wrong GDPR violations, poor public service, political backlash was as significant as not acting at all.

£80k+
Annual savings identified through targeted AI adoption
3,000+
Staff hours reclaimed per year
What was built

A 6-criteria prioritisation model scoring workflows against process complexity, volume, AI fit, and regulatory risk. FOI processing and complaints triage emerged as the highest-impact opportunities : clear, justifiable, defensible. A phased roadmap with embedded governance controls.

The key decision trade-off
We could have recommended broader automation. We didn't. We scoped tightly to the use cases where AI genuinely reduces risk rather than creating new risk vectors and documented why each rejected use case was rejected. That transparency is what makes a governance framework trustworthy.
What I'd do differently
Run a deeper staff sentiment survey earlier change management was underweighted at the start.
Build the monitoring framework in parallel, not after the roadmap so governance is baked in, not bolted on.

Risk & Controls · Governance · Deloitte Supervised

Enterprise Risk Framework : WB Alloys

Manufacturing SME · UK Multi-Site · Deloitte Engagement

Identified 85 previously unescalated safety risks buried in a single-point-of-failure system. Designed an ISO 31000-aligned framework with a centralised risk register, 5×5 scoring matrix, and three-tier governance structure, projecting a 74% reduction in reported safety risks.

85 risks surfaced 74% projected risk reduction ISO 31000 3-tier governance
↗
The problem

One person authorised all risk decisions across a multi-site UK manufacturing SME. 85 safety risks were completely unescalated not because management didn't care, but because the system had no language or structure to surface them. Glasgow accounted for 41% of all risk breaches.

85
Previously invisible safety risks identified
74%
Projected reduction in reported safety risks
What was built

Centralised risk register · 5×5 scoring matrix · 3-tier escalation structure (Branch → SMT → Board) · Shared risk taxonomy built from the client's own documentation across six categories. A 3-phase implementation roadmap with score-based escalation triggers.

The key decision trade-off
We built the taxonomy from the client's own language, not from ISO templates. This made adoption significantly easier but it meant more upfront qualitative work. The right trade-off: compliance frameworks fail when they feel foreign.
What I'd do differently
Pilot the 5×5 matrix with branch managers before finalising calibration sessions would have reduced subjectivity in scoring.
Pair the framework launch with a facilitated workshop at each site, not just documentation delivery.

Workforce Strategy · Operational Analytics · Deloitte

Workforce Strategy : UK Regional Police Force

Public Sector · UK Regional Police · Deloitte Engagement

Led quantitative FTE gap analysis across three regions, identifying approximately 28% understaffing in senior investigator roles. Crucially distinguished a skills and progression failure from a hiring problem, changing the entire strategic recommendation.

~28% understaffing diagnosed 3 regions Client-ready deliverable
↗
The problem

Operational bottlenecks were being attributed to a recruitment gap. The data told a different story: the problem wasn't that they weren't hiring enough people it was that existing talent wasn't progressing. That distinction completely changes what you recommend.

~28%
Understaffing in senior investigator roles the root cause
What was delivered

Structured rebalancing strategy covering digital upskilling pathways, accelerated promotion frameworks, and wellbeing initiatives. Delivered in client-ready format to senior stakeholders, with recommendations framed around what was actually causing the problem not what leadership had assumed.

The key decision trade-off
We had to push back on the initial brief. The client came in expecting a recruitment strategy. We reframed the problem around progression failure that required careful stakeholder management to be received well, not defensively. Worth it.
What I'd do differently
Involve frontline officers in the diagnostic earlier some progression barriers were cultural, not structural, and harder to see from data alone.

Data Analytics · Machine Learning · Life Sciences

Predictive Analytics : NHS A&E Early Warning System

Science Engineered · NHS England · Life Sciences & Healthcare

Built a Machine Learning early warning system for NHS A&E operations. Evaluated three classification models and selected Random Forest not for the highest accuracy, but for the lowest false negatives. In patient safety, missing a crisis is the worst possible outcome.

91% model accuracy Optimised for recall Full ML pipeline Board-ready output
↗
The problem

NHS A&E departments were managing crises reactively. The question wasn't "can we predict this?" it was "how do we translate complex model outputs into something a board can act on?" The technical work was only half the project.

91%
Model accuracy : Random Forest classifier
1/144
Missed crisis predictions
What was built

ML-ready dataset with 6 engineered features including lagged performance indicators and rolling averages. Time-based train/test split to prevent data leakage. Three algorithms evaluated. Key finding: A&E crisis builds gradually it is structurally predictable if you look at the right signals.

The key decision trade-off
Chose recall over accuracy. A false negative missing a crisis is catastrophically worse than a false positive in this context. That's a governance decision, not just a statistical one. The model selection was made with the end consequence in mind, not the leaderboard metric.
What I'd do differently
Work with clinical staff earlier to validate the feature selection some engineered features might not reflect operational reality as they understand it.
Build a live monitoring dashboard from the start, not just a static prediction output.

Sustainability Strategy · Procurement · ESG

Carbon Procurement Strategy : UK Residential Developer

Property Development · Sustainability Advisory

Built an LCA model quantifying 988 tCO₂e of embodied carbon across the supply chain. Designed an MCDA framework across three procurement scenarios, delivering a 34% emissions reduction recommendation that embedded sustainability as a weighted business criterion not an afterthought.

34% emissions reduction 988 tCO₂e baseline MCDA framework
↗
The insight

Sustainability risk is procurement risk. The challenge was making that argument legible to a property developer who saw sustainability as a compliance cost, not a strategic lever. The LCA model gave us the numbers. The MCDA gave us the framework to put ESG alongside cost and quality as an equally weighted input.

34%
Emissions reduction pathway identified
988
tCO₂e baseline quantified
What was built

Full Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) model across the supply chain. Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis framework integrating sustainability, cost, and quality across three procurement scenarios. Recommendation was specific, quantified, and grounded in the client's own commercial reality.

The key decision trade-off
Scenario 1 gave the deepest emissions cut but was commercially unviable. We recommended Scenario 2, a 34% reduction that the business could actually implement. Idealism without practicality doesn't get adopted.

Strategic Advisory · Financial Modelling · SE Asia

Business Model Feasibility : STAHL (~$190M Cost Base)

Strategic Advisory · South-East Asia

Prevented a high-risk strategic pivot through data-backed modelling. Built end-to-end P&L, ROI, and scenario analysis across a ~$190M cost base. The value was in making the risk of the wrong decision financially legible to leadership before they committed to it.

$190M cost base Multi-scenario P&L Strategic pivot prevented
↗
The problem

Leadership had already decided they wanted to pivot. Our job was to tell them whether that instinct was right with the financial modelling to back up whichever answer the data supported. The answer was: the pivot would fail. The hybrid model was the right path.

$190M
Cost base modelled across full P&L scenario analysis
What was delivered

End-to-end P&L model · Three-scenario stress test · ROI analysis across options · Hybrid model recommendation. The recommendation pushed back on leadership's preferred direction and was supported by data robust enough to make that conversation constructive rather than confrontational.

The key decision trade-off
Delivering a recommendation leadership doesn't want to hear is the hardest part of consulting. The only way to do it effectively is to make the evidence undeniable and the alternative clearly better which is what the hybrid model achieved.
What I'd do differently
Build the sensitivity analysis earlier and present it alongside the base case, not as a follow-up it makes the robustness of the recommendation more immediately credible.
How I work

The things that
don't show up in the work

01

Frame the problem
before touching data

The biggest consulting mistakes happen before the analysis starts when someone accepts the client's framing of the problem instead of stress-testing it. The NHS project found that A&E crises were structurally predictable. The police force project found that the real problem was progression failure, not recruitment. Neither of those insights came from the data. They came from asking the right question first.

Problem framing
02

Show the fork in the road,
not just the destination

Every project has a moment where multiple paths were equally reasonable. I document those moments deliberately what options existed, what trade-offs they carried, and why the chosen path was preferred. That's the evidence of critical thinking that gets lost when portfolios only show the polished final output. The trade-off IS the work.

Decision transparency
03

Constraints aren't
excuses — they're inputs

Every engagement had real constraints: limited data, tight timelines, clients who weren't sure what they actually needed. The public sector AI project had strict GDPR guardrails that ruled out the most technically exciting solutions. The STAHL project required a recommendation leadership didn't want to hear. Effective consulting means working with those constraints, not around them.
